# Embodied AI and Robotic Vision Group

The Embodied AI and Robotic Vision Group develops trustworthy perception and learning systems for autonomous robots operating in complex, uncertain, and changing environments. The group is led by Feras Dayoub at the Australian Institute for Machine Learning (AIML), Adelaide University.

* * *
# Research Focus

Our research connects robotic vision, machine learning, and embodied AI to help autonomous systems perceive, reason, adapt, and act safely in the real world.

Key interests include:

* **Trustworthy robotic perception**: monitoring perception systems, detecting failures, and estimating uncertainty during deployment.
* **Open-world and open-set perception**: enabling robots to recognize when they encounter unfamiliar objects, scenes, or operating conditions.
* **Vision-language and foundation models for robotics**: adapting modern AI models for robotic detection, manipulation, navigation, and scene understanding.
* **Long-term autonomy**: building systems that remain reliable as environments, tasks, and sensing conditions change over time.
* **Human-autonomous teaming**: developing intelligent systems that collaborate effectively with people in shared environments.

* * *
# Projects and Collaborations

The group contributes to research across autonomous robotics, robotic vision, and human-autonomous teaming, including work connected with the French-Australian CROSSING Lab (CNRS IRL), the Centre for Augmented Reasoning, and national and international collaborators.
